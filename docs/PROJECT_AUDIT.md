# Fig Tree Residences — Project Audit
### Phase 1A: Discovery & Asset Intelligence
Prepared as a discovery brief in advance of a luxury digital redesign. This document is the executive entry point to the eight companion reports in this folder; it does not make design recommendations, and no design, code, or UI work has been produced at this stage.

---

## 1. What This Phase Was

The instruction governing this work was explicit: understand the business before anything is designed. Every file inside the connected project folder was opened and read — thirty-seven unique images, a five-photograph record of the property's printed brochure, and the entirety of the live public website — and that material was supplemented with open web research to confirm the property's identity, address, and current digital footprint. No wireframes, mockups, components, or code were produced. The folders named `animations`, `design`, `deliverables`, and `website` that already exist in the project were found empty; they appear to be placeholders for later phases and have been left untouched.

## 2. What the Business Is

Fig Tree Residences is a boutique serviced-apartment property (an "aparthotel") at 13 Yacht Club Road, Msasani Peninsula, Masaki, Dar es Salaam, Tanzania, P.O. Box 1520. It sits on the Msasani Peninsula in what the property's own brochure calls "Leopard Cove Bay," roughly 200 metres from the Dar es Salaam Yacht Club and within a one-kilometre radius of the Slipway waterfront, Seacliff Village, the Aga Khan Clinic, SALI International Hospital, and two international schools. This is one of the most affluent, diplomatically and commercially dense residential districts in the city — a genuinely strong location asset that the current materials underuse.

The property opened to the market in a soft launch in May 2018, with all apartments operational by August 2018, per the site's own blog history. External aggregators (TripAdvisor, Booking.com, Hotels.com, Expedia) list it as a roughly 24-unit, four-star property. Its own marketing describes fully serviced, non-smoking studio apartments of about 43 m², each with a queen bed, sofa bed, private balcony, rain-shower bathroom, and a fully equipped kitchenette — positioning the product between a boutique hotel and a long-stay residence. Guest-facing amenities include an outdoor pool, an indoor and outdoor gym, a barbecue area set in mature gardens, a small private meeting room, a rooftop restaurant and bar with bay and sunset views, a grocery-shopping concierge service for self-caterers, secured parking, and a layered security system (coded room access, cameras, an electrified perimeter fence, and a response team).

## 3. Summary of Findings

The brand has real, usable raw material: a distinctive fig-tree logo mark, a consistent teal-and-terracotta colour language already applied to the physical building and printed collateral, a genuinely differentiated location story, and at least one high-quality, human, on-brand photograph (a smiling staff member in branded uniform). Several of the newest professional interior photographs show a well-designed, palm-motif arched headboard treatment that reads as considered and current.

Against that, the digital presence is thin and inconsistent. The live website (a Wix build at figtreeresidences.com) has not published room rates on its "Rooms & Rates" page, shows signs of copy-paste artifacts in its own page titles, mislabels its social icons, triplicates a payment button, and has produced only two blog posts in seven years. The photography library is small, inconsistently resolved (most images are compressed to roughly 1024 pixels wide — too low for large-format luxury use), contains at least four exact duplicate files, and includes a set of otherwise-strong newer photographs marred by a heavy yellow-green colour cast that needs correction before any of them could be used in a premium context. There is no brochure PDF on file — the only record of the printed brochure is five iPhone photographs of the physical booklet — and the master logo exists only as a photograph of a signboard, not as vector artwork.

None of this is a criticism of the property itself, which appears to be a well-run, well-located, well-reviewed business (four out of five on TripAdvisor, ranked in the upper half of Dar es Salaam hotels). It is a description of a digital asset base that has not kept pace with the physical product, and that is precisely the gap this discovery phase was commissioned to map.

## 4. How to Read the Rest of This Folder

`ASSET_INVENTORY.md` catalogues every file found, with format, resolution, and probable source. `PHOTO_AUDIT.md` grades the photography for redesign-readiness and flags the colour-cast and duplication issues in detail. `WEBSITE_ANALYSIS.md` is a page-by-page teardown of the live site. `CONTENT_DATABASE.md` is a verbatim transcription of every piece of marketing copy recovered from the brochure and the website. `BRAND_INTELLIGENCE.md` interprets the visual identity and voice. `COMPETITOR_NOTES.md` places the property against its immediate neighbourhood competitive set. `LUXURY_OPPORTUNITIES.md` synthesises the gaps above into a prioritised opportunity list. `NEXT_PHASE.md` sets out what needs to happen — and what needs to be asked of the client — before any design work begins.

## 5. A Note on Confidence

This audit was produced entirely from the files supplied and from public web sources; no interview with the property's owners, managers, or the Nextec Corp team responsible for the redesign has yet taken place. Several open questions — target guest profile, redesign budget, hosting and booking-engine preferences, and who currently owns the figtree.co.tz domain versus the figtreeresidences.com Wix site — are flagged in `NEXT_PHASE.md` rather than guessed at here.
