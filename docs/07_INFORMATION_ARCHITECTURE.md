# Fig Tree Residences — Information Architecture
### Phase 2: Experience Strategy — v1.0

This document defines what pages and sections the experience needs and why, and how they relate to one another. It is a structural map, not a visual one — no layout, navigation styling, or component design is specified here.

## 1. Principles Behind This Structure

The current site's navigation (Home, Rooms & Rates, Photo Gallery, Activities, About Us, Contact, Blog) is not fundamentally wrong — the categories are reasonable — but Discovery found real content missing inside several of them (no rates on "Rooms & Rates," no story on "About Us") and no structure at all for two things this phase has identified as important: a dedicated Location narrative and a Gardens narrative, both currently folded into other pages or missing entirely. The architecture below keeps what works, gives the two undersold assets their own space, and treats Tier One content from `06_CONTENT_HIERARCHY.md` as mandatory on every page it touches.

## 2. Proposed Page Structure

**Home** — the arrival moment. Its job is singular: convert search-stage curiosity into the feeling of having found somewhere real, using the property's best asset (the January 2025 drone photograph) and a small number of doorways into the rest of the site, not a compressed summary of every other page.

**Our Story** (replacing the current thin "About Us") — the brand narrative in full: the fig tree metaphor, the 2018 opening, the tranquility positioning, the Cicero line, and — once available — real staff presence. This page exists specifically to serve the "Discover Property" stage of the journey, which currently has nowhere to happen.

**Rooms** (replacing "Rooms & Rates" as a combined page) — genuine differentiation between room types, with real photography, real square-metreage, and real configuration detail per type, rather than one representative image standing in for the whole inventory.

**Rates & Booking** (split out as its own destination, not folded silently into the Rooms page) — the single most trust-critical page in the entire architecture, per Discovery's finding that no rates currently appear anywhere on the live site. This page should be reachable in one click from anywhere on the site, not buried behind a generic "Rooms & Rates" label that currently promises information it does not deliver.

**Amenities** — the practical reassurance page: pool, gym, BBQ gardens, conference room, rooftop, shopping concierge, and security, translated from the brochure's bullet list into the reassurance-first framing established in `06_CONTENT_HIERARCHY.md`.

**Gardens** (a new, dedicated page, not a subsection) — the property's photogenic living wall, mature trees, and outdoor seating deserve to be a destination in their own right, matching the "slowing down" moment identified as stage six of the guest journey.

**Location** (a new, dedicated page, elevated out of the brochure and given real weight) — the 200-metre distance to the Yacht Club, the walk to the Slipway, the proximity to international schools and clinics. Discovery identified this as the single strongest, most underused content asset in the entire project; it deserves a page of its own rather than a paragraph.

**Reviews** (a new page, or a prominent, permanent section rather than an absence) — bringing the property's existing 4-out-of-5 TripAdvisor standing and guest testimony onto the property's own site, where it can be framed on the property's own terms.

**Journal** (replacing "Blog," reframed as an ongoing practice rather than an occasional afterthought) — continuing the thread the October 2025 long-stay post already started, serving the Long-Stay Resident and Returning Guest personas directly.

**Contact** — kept, but corrected: a real address, a real map, a WhatsApp option (absent today despite its dominance as a booking channel in this market), and a contact form that does not falsely display a success message by default.

## 3. What Happens at Departure

`04_GUEST_JOURNEY.md` identified "Departure" as a stage with no digital touchpoint at all today. This architecture does not propose a literal new page for it — a full-page "goodbye" would work against the calm this brand is built on — but it flags that a post-stay moment (a follow-up email sequence, a returning-guest welcome state, or a Journal entry inviting reflection) should be scoped explicitly in a later phase rather than left, as it is today, entirely unaddressed.

## 4. Relationship Between Pages

Home should function as a single, unhurried gateway — offering a small number of clear paths (Our Story, Rooms, Location, Rates & Booking) rather than a long scroll trying to summarise everything at once. Gardens and Location, as the two new dedicated pages, should be reachable both from Home directly and from within Amenities and Our Story respectively, since both are referenced narratively elsewhere but deserve their own destination. Rates & Booking should be reachable from every single page in the architecture without exception, given its status as the highest-risk stage identified in `05_EXPERIENCE_MAP.md`.

## 5. What This Document Does Not Do

It does not specify navigation labels' visual treatment, menu behaviour, responsive collapsing, or any component design — those decisions belong to a later, explicitly visual phase of this project.
