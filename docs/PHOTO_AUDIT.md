# Fig Tree Residences — Photo Audit
### Phase 1A: Discovery & Asset Intelligence

This document grades the existing photography for its readiness to support a luxury digital redesign. It is a quality assessment only; it does not propose new shots, compositions, or edits.

## 1. Overall Verdict

The photography library is usable as a placeholder and as evidence of what the physical property looks like, but almost none of it is currently fit for a premium, large-format digital presentation. The single biggest asset-quality issue uncovered in this audit is a colour cast affecting the newest and otherwise best-composed photographs, described in detail below. The second biggest issue is resolution: with the exception of the five brochure snapshots (which were never meant to be marketing photography) and the eight newer AVIF images, every photograph in the project tops out at roughly 1024 pixels on its longest edge — adequate for a small web thumbnail, inadequate for a hero banner, a printed piece, or display on any screen larger than a phone.

## 2. The Colour-Cast Problem

Eight images — the AVIF files matching the property's own live Photo Gallery, including its two best-composed bedrooms (the arched palm-motif headboard rooms) and its best living-room shot — carry a strong, consistent yellow-green colour cast. Walls that are almost certainly white or cream in person render as a sickly chartreuse; skin tones and wood tones shift warm and muddy. This is not a stylistic choice — it appears in every image from this batch, including ones where a neutral white wall fills much of the frame — and reads as an uncorrected white-balance or colour-profile error introduced either at the time of capture or during export from whatever tool exported them to Wix's media library. These are simultaneously the property's newest, best-styled, and best-composed images, and they are also its most visibly broken from a colour-accuracy standpoint. Correcting or reshooting this set should be treated as a priority, not a nice-to-have, because it currently undermines photographs that would otherwise be the strongest assets in the library.

## 3. Resolution and Technical Quality

| Tier | Files | Longest edge | Fit for use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brochure photographs | 5 (`IMG_4611`–`IMG_4615`) | 4032 px | High resolution, but these are photos of a printed booklet, not original photography — useful only as a content-transcription source, not as final imagery |
| AVIF interior set | 8 | ~1054 px | Best composition in the library; undermined by the colour-cast issue above; still below the resolution needed for large hero use |
| Numbered web JPEGs | 23 (29 minus duplicates minus logo/brochure) | ~1024 px | Adequate for small web thumbnails only; visibly soft if enlarged past their native size |
| Logo | 1 | 1326 px | A photograph of a sign, not vector art; unusable for anything requiring clean edges at scale |

No image in the entire project exceeds roughly 1330 pixels on its longest edge with the exception of the brochure snapshots, which are photographs of paper rather than of the property. A luxury digital rebuild will need a genuinely new photography or professional re-scan pass; none of the existing library can be safely upscaled to modern hero-banner or print resolution without visible quality loss.

## 4. Duplicate and Near-Duplicate Content

Four exact-duplicate file pairs were identified and are listed in `ASSET_INVENTORY.md`. Beyond exact duplicates, there is meaningful content duplication at the scene level: at least three separate photographs of a living room feature the same sailboat/nautical painting and appear to be different angles of the same one or two rooms rather than a representative spread of distinct spaces, and the pool is photographed from four broadly similar angles with no single shot standing out as a definitive hero image. For a property with roughly 24 units, the guest-room photography is thin and repetitive relative to the amount of common-area photography — there is essentially one clearly differentiated "older-style" bedroom in the numbered set and two "newer-style" arched-headboard bedrooms in the AVIF set, which is not enough to reassure a prospective guest about what any specific room type actually looks like.

## 5. Strengths Worth Preserving

Three things in this library are genuinely good and should not be discarded in a redesign. First, the rooftop restaurant and bar photography taken at dusk and golden hour (`159693127`, `159693132`) is atmospheric, well-timed, and captures the bay-view selling point the brochure specifically promotes — this is the closest thing the library has to an emotionally compelling image. Second, the outdoor breakfast tray shot (`159693081`) is well styled, with fresh fruit, coffee, and juice arranged against a garden backdrop, and photographs like it are exactly what a boutique-residence audience responds to. Third, and most important from a brand standpoint, one AVIF photograph shows a smiling staff member in a Fig Tree–branded uniform — the only human being who appears anywhere in the entire asset library. A property whose brochure explicitly promises "a happy, bright and friendly collection of professionals" currently has almost no photographic evidence of its people; this single image should be treated as a seed to build on, not a curiosity.

## 6. Weak or Missing Photography

Kitchen and bathroom photography throughout the library is purely functional — clean, but composed like inventory documentation rather than lifestyle marketing. The gym photograph is a straightforward equipment shot with no sense of use or atmosphere. The reception and lobby photographs are dominated by two framed portraits of Tanzanian public figures on the wall, which is a common and unremarkable choice in East African office and hospitality interiors, but which competes visually with the branded environment in a small frame and deserves a deliberate styling decision rather than being left as an incidental background detail. There is no photography at all of the security features the property markets prominently (coded access, perimeter fencing, monitored cameras), no night exterior photography, no wide establishing/aerial shot at usable resolution, no photography of the conference room the brochure lists as an amenity, and no video or motion content of any kind.

## 7. Recommendation Summary for This Phase

No new photography should be commissioned or generated as part of this discovery phase — that decision belongs to the next phase, once the client has confirmed budget and scope. What this audit establishes is the baseline: the existing library can support a placeholder or interim site, but a genuine luxury redesign will require, at minimum, colour correction or reshoot of the eight AVIF images, a proper hero/exterior photograph at full resolution, a representative photograph of every distinct room type the property actually sells, and a modest amount of authentic staff/human photography to support the brand's own stated warmth-and-service positioning.
