# Fig Tree Residences — Guest Journey
### Phase 2: Experience Strategy — v1.0

The full journey, from first search to post-stay review, with the emotion the experience should produce at each step and the risk currently sitting at that step per Discovery's findings. This is an emotional and structural map, not a page-by-page design spec.

## 1. Google Search

**Emotion to produce:** Curiosity edged with relief — "this doesn't look like every other hotel result."
**Current risk:** Every third-party listing (TripAdvisor, Booking.com, Expedia) categorises Fig Tree as a hotel among 139 competitors, and the site's own meta titles carry a visible copy-paste artifact ("Www.figtree.co.tz" as literal text). A search result should read as distinctive even in a dense, low-attention list of alternatives; right now it reads as one more line in that list.

## 2. Homepage

**Emotion to produce:** Arrival — the feeling of stepping through a gate into somewhere quieter than where they just were.
**Current risk:** The homepage's actual best asset, a January 2025 drone photograph, is genuinely strong; everything beneath it currently competes for attention with an unfinished FAQ accordion and marketing language identical to what the visitor just left on Google. The emotional job of this single screen is disproportionate to its content: it must convert curiosity into the feeling of having found somewhere, before a single amenity is explained.

## 3. Discover Property

**Emotion to produce:** Recognition — "this is a real place with a real story," not a template.
**Current risk:** The About page currently contains two generic paragraphs and omits the property's own most interesting facts (the 2018 opening story, the fig-tree name's meaning, the Cicero line on the brochure). This is the step where the brand narrative from `02_BRAND_STORY.md` needs to be told in full for the first time — right now, nothing tells it at all.

## 4. Explore Rooms

**Emotion to produce:** Specificity — "I can picture the exact room I would be in," not a generic composite.
**Current risk:** Discovery found essentially one clearly differentiated older-style room and two newer arched-headboard rooms (visually compromised by a colour cast) standing in for roughly twenty-four units, with no rates and no named room categories. This step currently asks a guest to imagine, rather than see, what they're booking.

## 5. Amenities

**Emotion to produce:** Reassurance — a sense that every practical need has already been anticipated.
**Current risk:** The amenities list itself (pool, gym, BBQ gardens, conference room, rooftop, shopping concierge, security) is strong and specific; the problem is presentation, not substance — it currently reads as a bulleted checklist rather than a set of small, considered comforts. The security detail in particular — nine specific measures, more than any other category in the brochure — is currently the least emotionally translated fact in the entire project.

## 6. Gardens

**Emotion to produce:** Slowing down — the single moment in the journey where pacing itself should visibly change.
**Current risk:** The gardens exist and are genuinely photogenic (a green living wall, mature trees, rattan seating, a poolside bench with a magazine and juice already staged), but nothing on the current site treats the gardens as a destination in their own right rather than a backdrop to other photographs.

## 7. Location

**Emotion to produce:** Confidence — "everything I need is close, and I am somewhere genuinely good."
**Current risk:** The brochure's own location copy and map (200 metres from the Yacht Club, a five-minute walk to the Slipway waterfront, minutes from two international schools and two medical clinics) is the single strongest, most specific piece of content in the entire project, and it currently appears nowhere on the live website in this level of detail.

## 8. Reviews

**Emotion to produce:** Validation — "other people like me have trusted this place, and been right to."
**Current risk:** No review content of any kind currently appears on the property's own site; its 4-out-of-5 TripAdvisor standing exists entirely off-property, where a visitor has to go looking for it themselves rather than being shown it at the moment of decision.

## 9. Booking

**Emotion to produce:** Ease — the last possible moment for friction to appear, and the moment it currently does.
**Current risk:** No visible rates, a triplicated and confusing payment-icon block, and no clear digital path to completing a booking without a phone call. This is the single highest-risk step in the entire journey today, because every emotional gain made in steps one through eight is spent the instant a visitor cannot simply book.

## 10. Arrival

**Emotion to produce:** Confirmation — "the promise made online is being kept in person."
**Current risk:** Outside this project's scope to influence directly (it is an operational, not digital, moment), but the digital experience's job is to set expectations precisely enough that arrival feels like confirmation rather than surprise in either direction.

## 11. Stay

**Emotion to produce:** Settling — the shift from guest to (briefly) resident.
**Current risk:** Also largely operational, but the digital experience can support this by ensuring long-stay content (laundry, kitchen, garden life, the property's own "home away from home" language) was set up honestly during discovery, so the lived reality matches what was promised rather than under- or over-selling it.

## 12. Departure

**Emotion to produce:** Reluctance — the literal target state named in this phase's mission ("I never want to leave").
**Current risk:** There is currently no digital touchpoint at all for this moment — no thank-you sequence, no invitation to return, nothing that acknowledges the guest is leaving. This is an entirely open opportunity, not a fix.

## 13. Review

**Emotion to produce:** Advocacy — turning a guest into a voice for the property, not just a completed transaction.
**Current risk:** With only two blog posts in seven years and no visible review-solicitation on-site, the property currently has no active mechanism for turning a good stay into public evidence of that stay, leaving its entire review reputation to accumulate passively on third-party OTA pages it does not control the framing of.

## 14. What This Journey Establishes for Later Phases

The emotional arc this journey describes — curiosity, arrival, recognition, specificity, reassurance, slowing down, confidence, validation, ease, confirmation, settling, reluctance, advocacy — is the spine `05_EXPERIENCE_MAP.md` and `08_STORYTELLING_FLOW.md` build on. Steps 9 (Booking) and 12 (Departure) carry the most risk and the most opportunity respectively, and should be treated as priorities when this strategy is eventually translated into structure and design.
