# Fig Tree Residences — Competitor Notes
### Phase 1A: Discovery & Asset Intelligence

This document records the competitive names that surfaced directly from the project's own materials and from confirming web research. It is a discovery-stage map of the neighbourhood competitive set, not a full competitive-positioning study — deep audits of each competitor's own site, pricing, and digital presence were out of scope for this phase and are recommended as a distinct next-phase task in `NEXT_PHASE.md`.

## 1. Competitors Named by the Property's Own Materials

The brochure's location page includes an annotated map showing Fig Tree Residences alongside nearby hospitality and lifestyle venues. Two of these are direct accommodation competitors on the same peninsula:

- **Best Western Coral Beach** — an international chain-branded beach hotel appearing on the same map, within the same walking-distance radius the brochure uses to sell its own location.
- **Sea Cliff** — appears on the map as a landmark; Sea Cliff is a well-known hotel and lifestyle precinct on the Msasani Peninsula and is one of the most established hospitality names in this specific neighbourhood.
- **DoubleTree by Hilton Hotel Dar es Salaam** — an international-chain-branded hotel also captured on the brochure's own map, representing a materially larger and more heavily resourced competitor within the same immediate radius.

The same map also names a cluster of restaurants and bars that function as amenity competitors for the rooftop dining experience Fig Tree markets — Epi d'Or, Marrybrown, Grill House Dar es Salaam, Akira Lounge & Bar, The Slow Leopard, and George and Dragon — none of which are accommodation competitors, but all of which compete for the same "where do I eat tonight in Masaki" decision that Fig Tree's own rooftop bar is trying to capture.

## 2. A Competitor Found by Coincidence, Not by Research

One of the five brochure photographs supplied for this audit (`IMG_4613.jpg`) shows a Serena Hotels–branded mousepad sitting on the same desk as the Fig Tree brochure, alongside what appears to be a Grant Thornton–branded item in a later photograph (`IMG_4614.jpg`). Serena Hotels is a significant, well-capitalised East African luxury hospitality group with a strong presence in Tanzania. Its appearance here is almost certainly incidental — these brochure photographs were evidently taken on someone's desk, not staged for competitive research — but it is worth surfacing as a data point: Serena's branded materials are physically circulating in the same professional environment (plausibly an accounting, audit, or consulting office, given the Grant Thornton item) where Fig Tree's own brochure was being reviewed. If Serena Hotels operates a boutique or serviced-residence product in Dar es Salaam, it should be treated as a benchmark for what a well-resourced regional operator's brand execution looks like, and included explicitly in a future competitive-positioning study.

## 3. Third-Party Channel Competitive Set

Fig Tree Residences is listed and ranked on TripAdvisor at position 60 of 139 hotels in Dar es Salaam, with a 4-out-of-5 rating — meaning its true competitive set, from a discovery and booking-decision standpoint, is not just the handful of properties on its own brochure map but the full roster of roughly 139 accommodation listings a prospective guest sees when comparing options for the city. This audit did not attempt to review that full list; it is flagged here as the honest scope of the competitive landscape, distinct from the small, curated set of neighbours the brochure itself chooses to acknowledge.

## 4. What This Phase Did Not Do

No competitor website was fetched, no competitor pricing was gathered, no competitor photography, review sentiment, or social presence was reviewed, and no formal SWOT or positioning matrix has been produced. Given how much of Fig Tree's own positioning (serviced-apartment, long-stay, privacy-and-security) depends on how it reads against Sea Cliff, Coral Beach, DoubleTree, and Serena specifically, a dedicated competitive-intelligence pass against those four named properties is the most valuable single next step this document can recommend, and it is carried forward as a specific action item in `NEXT_PHASE.md`.
