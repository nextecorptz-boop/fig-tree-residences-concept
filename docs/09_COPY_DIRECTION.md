# Fig Tree Residences — Copy Direction
### Phase 2: Experience Strategy — v1.0

This document sets voice, tone, and language direction for all future copywriting. It is a set of principles and examples for direction-setting, not final website copy — actual page-by-page copy is a later, separate deliverable.

## 1. The Voice, in One Line

Fig Tree should read like a knowledgeable, unhurried host speaking to a guest they already respect — never like a marketing department, and never like a hotel chain's brand-voice template. Discovery's brand analysis found the current site's actual written voice is already warm and personal, just inconsistently executed (spelling slips, uneven capitalisation, generic filler alongside genuinely good lines). The direction here is to keep the warmth and fix the inconsistency, not to replace the voice with something more "polished" and less human.

## 2. Three Words to Write Toward

**Calm.** Sentences should be allowed to be short and simple. Avoid stacking adjectives ("stunning, breathtaking, unforgettable") — the Cicero line the brand already owns proves that one precise, quiet sentence outperforms five excited ones.

**Specific.** Every claim should be a fact, not an adjective. "200 metres from the Dar es Salaam Yacht Club" is stronger copy than "prime location" will ever be, because it is true, checkable, and unique to this property. Discovery found the brochure already does this well in its location and room-feature copy; the live website's marketing language ("comfort and style in soft relaxing ocean colours") does it poorly. New copy should follow the brochure's example, not the website's.

**Rooted.** Language should draw, lightly and without overexplaining, on the fig-tree metaphor established in `02_BRAND_STORY.md` — growth, shelter, roots, seasons, patience — without turning it into a gimmick repeated on every page. It should surface once, said plainly, and then let the rest of the copy simply behave consistently with it.

## 3. Words and Patterns to Avoid

- **"Hotel."** The brand's own positioning, and every persona in `03_GUEST_PERSONAS.md`, depends on this being a residence, not a hotel. The word should not appear in first-party copy.
- **Superlative stacking** ("world-class," "unparalleled," "breathtaking") — these are the default vocabulary of every competing hospitality website and carry no differentiating information.
- **Urgency language** ("book now," "limited availability," countdown-style phrasing) — directly at odds with the calm this brand is built on, and with the storytelling flow's Act Five principle that booking should feel like an easy conclusion, not a push.
- **Unverified spelling and copy-paste artifacts** — the current site's "Accomodation," "aray," the Cicero misattribution ("Tulius" for "Tullius"), and the literal fragment "Www.figtree.co.tz" inside page titles are small, avoidable failures that undercut every larger effort at a considered, luxury voice.

## 4. Tone Calibration by Persona

Discovery found all six personas converge on the same three underlying brand pillars (privacy, security, calm), but each persona hears them differently, and copy should flex emphasis, not voice, accordingly: security-focused copy should sound like quiet dependability for Embassy Staff, like child-safety reassurance for the Family, and like operational confidence for the Business Traveler — the same fact, three different points of emphasis, always in the same calm voice.

## 5. Example Direction — Before and After

Current live-site copy: *"Our apartments are fully stocked complete with kitchen and balcony. Comfort and style in soft relaxing ocean colours. Spacious generously appointed rooms."*

Direction for replacement copy should read closer to: *"Each residence has its own kitchen, its own balcony, its own quiet. Forty-three square metres, arranged the way a home is arranged — not a hotel room."* This is not a final copy deliverable; it is an illustration of the shift from generic adjective-stacking to specific, calm, ownable language, built directly from facts Discovery already confirmed (43 m², kitchenette, balcony) rather than invented ones.

## 6. The Cicero Line's Job

*"The pursuit, even of the best things, ought to be calm and tranquil."* This line currently exists only on the printed brochure cover and appears nowhere on the live site. Copy direction going forward should treat it as the brand's signature statement — used sparingly, likely once, in a position of real weight (the homepage, or the close of the Our Story page) rather than repeated as a slogan. A signature line loses its power the moment it is used often enough to become wallpaper.

## 7. How This Direction Should Be Used

Any future copywriter or content phase should check draft copy against Sections 2 and 3 above before checking it against anything else — a well-written sentence that reintroduces "hotel," urgency, or superlative stacking is a direction failure regardless of its grammatical quality.
