# Fig Tree Residences — Experience Map
### Phase 2: Experience Strategy — v1.0

Where `04_GUEST_JOURNEY.md` walks through the journey narratively, this document lays the same fourteen stages out as a single cross-referenced map — emotional target, dominant sense, which persona each stage matters most to, and current risk level — so later phases can see at a glance where effort is best spent.

## 1. The Map

| Stage | Primary Emotion | Dominant Sense/Content Focus | Persona It Matters Most To | Current Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Google Search | Curiosity, relief | Distinctiveness in a list | Business Traveler, Embassy Staff | Medium — buried among 139 "hotel" listings |
| Homepage | Arrival | Sight (the drone hero image) | All personas | High — best asset undercut by unfinished elements below it |
| Discover Property | Recognition | Story, voice | Returning Guest, Long-Stay Resident | High — no narrative currently exists |
| Explore Rooms | Specificity | Sight, spatial imagination | Family, Business Traveler | High — one image standing in for ~24 units, no rates |

## 2. Reading the Map: Where the Real Leverage Is

Three stages carry disproportionate weight relative to how little current attention they receive. **Location** is the single strongest asset in the entire project — a specific, verifiable, genuinely enviable set of facts (200 metres to the Yacht Club, walking distance to international schools and clinics) — and it currently exists in more depth in a printed brochure photographed on someone's desk than anywhere on the live site. **Booking** is the stage every other stage's emotional work is spent to reach, and it is currently the point of maximum friction in the entire journey — a guest who has been won over by everything before it can still fail to book simply because no rate is visible. **Departure** is named directly in this phase's own mission statement ("I never want to leave") and yet is the one stage with no digital presence whatsoever today — nothing invites a guest back, nothing acknowledges the moment is ending. Any future prioritisation exercise should weight these three stages above the others.

## 3. Reading the Map: Persona Concentration

Embassy Staff and the Business Traveler appear most often in the "matters most" column for the earliest, most rational stages (search, location, booking) — these are personas moving quickly and needing clarity fast. The Long-Stay Resident and Returning Guest concentrate at the later, more emotional stages (gardens, departure, review) — these are personas who have already decided speed is not the priority, and who reward depth and continuity instead. The Family and NGO Professional sit in the middle of the map, needing both practical clarity (rooms, amenities) and emotional reassurance (gardens, security) in roughly equal measure. This distribution is a useful check for later content and information-architecture decisions: content built purely for speed will under-serve the second half of the map, and content built purely for atmosphere will under-serve the first half.

## 4. What This Map Does Not Do

This map does not assign specific pages, layouts, or visual treatments to any stage — that begins in `07_INFORMATION_ARCHITECTURE.md` and is explicitly deferred past this phase entirely for anything visual. Its only job is to make the emotional and risk landscape visible in one place before those structural decisions are made.
