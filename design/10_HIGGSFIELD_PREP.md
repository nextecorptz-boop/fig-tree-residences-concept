# Fig Tree Residences — Higgsfield Production Prep
### Phase 3: Luxury Design Language — v2.0

This document is a production brief for every major section of the site, written so that AI-assisted image and video generation (or a real shoot briefed the same way) produces material consistent with everything set out in `01_DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md` through `09_VISUAL_DIRECTION.md`. Each section specifies scene, emotion, lighting, camera movement, lens, duration, transition, sound mood, and animation/video priority. Nothing in this document is itself a generated asset — it is the brief used to produce one.

## 1. Hero (Home)

- **Scene:** The property from above, garden and teal rooftop visible against the Msasani coastline, morning light — extending the property's own existing January 2025 drone photograph rather than replacing its concept.
- **Emotion:** Arrival; the first held breath.
- **Lighting:** Soft, early-morning natural light, warm but not golden-hour saturated — clarity over drama.
- **Camera movement:** A single, extremely slow drift forward (drone push-in, near-imperceptible), no orbit, no swoop.
- **Lens:** Wide, but not fisheye-distorted — natural aerial perspective.
- **Duration:** 8–12 seconds, looped seamlessly if used as a subtle motion background behind a static headline; otherwise, a single still frame is an entirely acceptable and arguably preferable substitute per `06_MOTION_LANGUAGE.md`'s instruction that video should only replace stills where movement adds real meaning.
- **Transition:** Hard cut to the next section is acceptable here specifically, since the hero itself should feel held and still rather than actively transitioning away.
- **Sound mood:** If sound is used at all (muted by default, opt-in only, per standard autoplay-audio restraint): distant, soft ocean and wind ambience — no music.
- **Animation priority:** Low — this section should rely on stillness, not motion, to do its work.
- **Video priority:** Low-to-medium — a strong still image fulfills this brief; video is a refinement, not a requirement.

## 2. Our Story

- **Scene:** A slow, intimate interior/exterior sequence — morning light through the garden, a hand on the rattan furniture, the fig-tree logo signboard, a staff member arranging the rooftop tables before service.
- **Emotion:** Recognition; being told a true story by someone who knows it well.
- **Lighting:** Warm, soft, slightly diffused — the "quiet luxury" register throughout, never harsh or high-contrast.
- **Camera movement:** Gentle, handheld-feeling drift (not a locked tripod, not a swooping crane) — should feel like a person walking through the space, not a drone or a dolly rig.
- **Lens:** A standard, human-perspective focal length (roughly 35–50mm equivalent) — deliberately avoiding wide-angle distortion per `09_VISUAL_DIRECTION.md`.
- **Duration:** 15–20 seconds if produced as a single narrative sequence; may also be delivered as a set of 3–4 still frames if video is not prioritised for this section.
- **Transition:** Slow cross-fade between shots (matching `motion-reveal`, 700ms, in spirit even though this is pre-rendered footage, not interface motion).
- **Sound mood:** Warm, minimal acoustic texture if any — no vocal narration required; this section's storytelling is carried by on-screen copy, not a voiceover.
- **Animation priority:** Medium.
- **Video priority:** Medium — this is the section where authentic motion (a person, real light changing) adds the most credibility per Phase 2's Brand Story emphasis on real people and a real place.

## 3. Rooms

- **Scene:** Each distinct room type, shot as its own sequence — the arched palm-headboard studio and the older-style room treated as genuinely separate scenes, not one generic "room" concept reused.
- **Emotion:** Specificity; "I can see myself here."
- **Lighting:** Bright, clean, colour-accurate daylight — this is the section most directly correcting Phase 1's colour-cast finding, so lighting accuracy is the single highest priority of this brief.
- **Camera movement:** Minimal — a slow, level pan across the room at most, or a static frame; no movement that could exaggerate or distort perceived room size.
- **Lens:** Standard focal length, matched consistently across every room so guests can honestly compare room types against each other.
- **Duration:** 6–10 seconds per room if animated; stills are an acceptable and often preferable primary format for this section, per the Room Experience component's need for a clear, comparable, non-distorted reference image.
- **Transition:** Simple cross-fade when moving between room types in a gallery context.
- **Sound mood:** None — this section is informational within an emotional frame, not atmospheric.
- **Animation priority:** Low.
- **Video priority:** Low — accuracy and consistency outrank cinematic motion here.

## 4. Amenities

- **Scene:** Short, individual vignettes per amenity — the pool at rest, the gym in soft morning light, the BBQ garden set for evening use, the rooftop bar being prepared.
- **Emotion:** Reassurance; anticipated comfort.
- **Lighting:** Consistent daylight-neutral grading matching Section 3 — no dramatic mood lighting that would make amenities feel inconsistent with the Rooms section.
- **Camera movement:** Static or a very slight, slow push-in — enough to feel alive, not enough to distract from the amenity itself.
- **Lens:** Standard focal length.
- **Duration:** 4–6 seconds per amenity if animated.
- **Transition:** Simple cut or short cross-fade between amenities in sequence.
- **Sound mood:** None.
- **Animation priority:** Low.
- **Video priority:** Low-to-medium — stills are sufficient for most amenities; brief motion is most valuable for the pool (gentle water movement) specifically.

## 5. Garden Experience

- **Scene:** The living wall, mature trees, dappled light through leaves, a bench with a book or drink already staged, per the property's own existing garden photography.
- **Emotion:** Slowing down — this section's entire purpose per `04_COMPONENT_LIBRARY.md`.
- **Lighting:** Soft, filtered, natural — the specific quality of light passing through leaves, not flat, even daylight.
- **Camera movement:** The single slowest, most deliberate movement in the entire brief — a very gentle drift or almost-static frame with only natural movement in the scene itself (leaves shifting slightly, light flickering).
- **Lens:** Standard-to-slightly-longer focal length for a soft, intimate compression, avoiding any wide-angle grandeur that would undercut the section's quiet, personal register.
- **Duration:** 10–15 seconds, the longest held single shot in the system, deliberately slow to watch.
- **Transition:** A long, slow fade to and from black or to the surrounding cream background — no hard cuts entering or leaving this section.
- **Sound mood:** If used: birdsong, leaves, distant wind — the most atmospheric sound treatment in the entire brief, matching this section's role as the emotional low-pace anchor of the whole site.
- **Animation priority:** Medium — subtle, natural motion (leaves, light) matters more here than in any other section.
- **Video priority:** High — this is the one section where genuine, gentle motion most directly serves the brand's emotional thesis ("I never want to leave").

## 6. Restaurant (Rooftop)

- **Scene:** Golden-hour and dusk service — string lights, the bay view, a table being set, extending the property's own strongest existing photography.
- **Emotion:** Warm anticipation; the day's most special moment.
- **Lighting:** Golden hour into blue-hour dusk, warm string-light practicals visible in frame — matching the `color-surface-dusk` register defined in `03_DESIGN_TOKENS.md`.
- **Camera movement:** A slow, low, gentle push toward the view (bay/Yacht Club) or across a set table — should feel like settling into a seat, not a sweeping establishing shot.
- **Lens:** Standard-to-slightly-wide, enough to include both the intimate table detail and the view beyond it in the same frame.
- **Duration:** 10–15 seconds.
- **Transition:** Slow cross-fade, matching the section's own warm "breathing" motion treatment described in `06_MOTION_LANGUAGE.md`.
- **Sound mood:** Distant ambient evening sound — soft conversation murmur, glassware, light breeze — no overt music track.
- **Animation priority:** Medium — the section's `motion-ambient` treatment (string lights swaying, light flicker) should feel alive without looping obviously.
- **Video priority:** High — alongside Gardens, this is the property's most emotionally proven existing content, and it deserves the same production priority.

## 7. Location

- **Scene:** A short sequence moving from the property's own gate or garden edge out toward the Yacht Club and Slipway waterfront, grounding the specific proximity claims from Phase 1's Content Database in something visual rather than only a map.
- **Emotion:** Confidence; specific, verifiable closeness.
- **Lighting:** Clear, bright daylight — clarity over atmosphere in this section specifically, since its job is informational trust.
- **Camera movement:** A single, steady walking-pace movement (or drone flyover at low altitude) tracing one of the brochure's specific distances — for example, the 200 metres to the Yacht Club — literally covering that ground on screen.
- **Lens:** Standard.
- **Duration:** 8–10 seconds.
- **Transition:** Simple cut to the custom-styled map described in `04_COMPONENT_LIBRARY.md`.
- **Sound mood:** Ambient street/coastal sound, kept low.
- **Animation priority:** Low.
- **Video priority:** Medium — a well-produced still map paired with strong photography can fulfil this section without video; video adds credibility if resources allow.

## 8. Departure / Footer Moment

- **Scene:** A quiet, closing image — the garden gate, a bag by the door, the view from the rooftop one last time at dusk — deliberately the visual bookend to the Hero's opening image.
- **Emotion:** Reluctance; "I never want to leave," addressed directly, for the first time anywhere in this project's digital presence.
- **Lighting:** Warm, late-day light, echoing the Restaurant section's register to close the visual loop the Hero opened.
- **Camera movement:** Static or an extremely slow pull-back, the visual opposite of the Hero's slow push-in — the experience should feel like it is gently letting go, not cutting off.
- **Lens:** Standard.
- **Duration:** 5–8 seconds, or a single still frame — this is a small, quiet closing gesture, not a major set-piece.
- **Transition:** A slow fade to the footer's calm, information-dense resting state.
- **Sound mood:** None, or the same soft ambient tone as the Hero, closing the loop.
- **Animation priority:** Low.
- **Video priority:** Low — this is an entirely new moment for the brand (per Phase 2's finding that Departure has no digital touchpoint today), and a single well-chosen still image is enough to establish it before investing in motion.

## 9. Cross-Section Production Notes

Every brief above should be produced or generated using the exact colour values in `03_DESIGN_TOKENS.md` as a grading reference, and every finished asset should be checked against the neutral-wall test described in `09_VISUAL_DIRECTION.md` before approval. Sound, where used at all, is always optional and muted by default across every section — nothing in this system should autoplay audio at a visitor. Gardens and Restaurant are the two sections where video investment should be prioritised first if resources are limited, since they build most directly on the property's own already-proven strongest photography; Rooms and Amenities are lowest priority for motion specifically because accuracy and comparability matter more there than atmosphere.
